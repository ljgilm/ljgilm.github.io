STEM 8th Grade

Over the year, students explored engineering and computer science through three major projects: creating interactive Choose-Your-Own Adventure Twine games, designing and building functional mechanical hands, and developing original video games using the Unity Game Engine. They practiced the Engineering Design Process throughout the year and concluded with a showcase of their Unity games to the Upper School STEM class and fellow faculty from the STEM department and tech theater.

VR Game Camp

In this exciting camp, students got a hands-on introduction to game and virtual reality (VR) development. They explored everything from game design basics to building 3D models in Blender, coding in C#, and creating their own VR experiences using the Unity game engine. To help them get started, I created a custom VR project template that showed how key features, like player movement and object interaction, work. With that foundation, students jumped into making their own unique VR games using the template as a base. The camp wrapped up with a fun showcase where they proudly shared their creations with friends and family!
