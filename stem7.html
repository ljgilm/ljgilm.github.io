STEM 7th Grade

During a semester of STEM, 7th graders were introduced to the Engineering Design Process (EDP). They explored all the steps in the EDP, with a focus on research, brainstorming, and planning. One major project involved creating digital animated storybooks in Scratch and physical interactive children's storybooks. Before building the physical storybooks, students combined blueprints and storyboards into a single document to map out their ideas and guide them. They outlined the structure and design of their storybook, while also planning the flow, interactivity, and visuals of their stories. As they worked on their projects, students had the opportunity to express their creativity through various mediums, including artistic design, coding, sound design, and even voice acting. While learning engineering and computer science concepts, this project encouraged students to integrate their diverse abilities into their final designs.


Interactive Children's Storybooks

7th Graders researched about motion and different types of motions and mechanisms. Then, afterwards, they created storyboards and blueprints for their stories before finally creating the physical prototypes of their designs.

Animated Children's Storybooks with Scrath

The students researched about their stories. Then, they created storyboards before following tutorials on how to bring their story to life on Scratch. The students created their own artwork while also block coding and adding music and/or voice acting/narration.
