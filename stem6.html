STEM 6th Grade

Within a semester, 6th graders were introduced to the Engineering Design Process. They gradually learned research and blueprinting skills by developing dog parks, dog shelters, or special rooms for 'clients'. In groups, they designed and created 3D models of their designs using LEGOs. With these design skills under their belts, they created their own worlds on a gridded map, defining the rules and parameters that guided players from start to finish, while exploring introductory computer science concepts. They then played the games designed by their classmates, provided feedback, and even combined some worlds together. For the final STEM unit, students learned about VEX robotics through small competitions, coding a robot to draw, and for their culminating project, they designed and built obstacle courses for their robots that they built as a team.


Dog Shelter/Park & Clients LEGO Models

6th Graders researched about the requirements and characteristics of Dog Parks and Shelters and hypothetical 'clients'. The students then designed and built their plans using LEGOs.

Create Your Own World - Game Board

The students created their own worlds on a gridded map. They created the rules in their world that would determine whether the players won or lost. This project was about introducing them to sequential steps and the idea of conditions such as having traps or the player needing to gain information before they can go further. By creating their own game and utilizing game design, the students learned about basic conceptps in computer science along with preparing them for when they will build and direct their VEX robots.

VEX Robotics - Obstacle Course

The 6th Graders designed obstacle courses for their robots while also incorporating a Rube Goldberg aspect to the course. Students either drove their robot based on certain criteria for the maze or designed a maze and coded their robot to complete the course.
