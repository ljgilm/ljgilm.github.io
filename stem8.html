STEM 8th Grade

STEM for 8th grade is a year-long course that exposed students to both engineering and computer science while providing ample opportunities to practice the Engineering Design Process. Throughout the year, students worked on three major projects. The first project involved creating their own digital Choose-Your-Own-Adventure game online using Twine. They were introduced to fundamental computer science concepts, such as conditional statements, and then applied their learning by creating decision trees and flowcharts using Canva. Next, students researched, designed, and iterated on their Functional Mechanical Hands. They started with paper prototypes and then progressed to building hands from durable, recyclable materials, testing how well they functioned like a real hand. The final project was a group effort that introduced students to game development. They created and pitched their own games, then built them using the Unity Game Engine. At the end of the year, students showcased their games at a special event, presenting their projects to the Upper School STEM class and other faculty members.

Building a Mechanical Hand

The students created their own worlds on a gridded map. They created the rules in their world that would determine whether the players won or lost. This project was about introducing them to sequential steps and the idea of conditions such as having traps or the player needing to gain information before they can go further. By creating their own game and utilizing game design, the students learned about basic conceptps in computer science along with preparing them for when they will build and direct their VEX robots.




Unity Game Development

The 6th Graders designed obstacle courses for their robots while also incorporating a Rube Goldberg aspect to the course. Students either drove their robot based on certain criteria for the maze or designed a maze and coded their robot to complete the course.
