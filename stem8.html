STEM 8th Grade

STEM for 8th grade is a year-long course that exposed students to both engineering and computer science while providing ample opportunities to practice the Engineering Design Process. Throughout the year, students worked on three major projects. The first project involved creating their own digital Choose-Your-Own-Adventure game online using Twine. They were introduced to fundamental computer science concepts, such as conditional statements, and then applied their learning by creating decision trees and flowcharts using Canva. Next, students researched, designed, and iterated on their Functional Mechanical Hands. They started with paper prototypes and then progressed to building hands from durable, recyclable materials, testing how well they functioned like a real hand. The final project was a group effort that introduced students to game development. They created and pitched their own games, then built them using the Unity Game Engine. At the end of the year, students showcased their games at a special event, presenting their projects to the Upper School STEM class and other faculty members.


Choose Your Own Adventure Twine Story: Game Development & Computer Science

The 8th Graders created their own digital Choose-Your-Own-Adventure game online using Twine. They were introduced to fundamental computer science concepts, such as conditional statements, and then applied their learning by creating decision trees and flowcharts using Canva. Once the students developed their decision tree/ flow chart for their CYOA game, they turned it into interactive online game through Twine or for the few that wanted to tackle a Narratice Scripting Language through Inkle.

Building a Mechanical Hand

Through the Engineering Design Process, the students researched about the anatomy of the hand and how it functions. Afterwards, the students designed paper prototypes of a functional hand, and then created a durable functional hand with multiple iterations throughout the entire process. By the end, they tested to see how functional their hands were with test like catching a ball, picking up a marker, etc.

Unity Game Development

The students were introduced to Unity Game Engine and how it works. After presenting individual and group game pitches, they worked together in pairs to create 2D or 3D digital games. The purpose of this project was to expose the students to the Unity software and how coding can create a video game. Students created games using a template created by the teacher and customized it or were provided a resource guide for specific goals they wanted to achieve in their game.
